    1. General Terms Copy

    1.1. When you access the Site, we utilize cookies and similar technologies to either retrieve or store information on the User's device.

    1.2. You can find out more about cookies and other similar technologies and how to manage them in the information below.

    2. What is cookie and local storage? Copy

    Cookies are simple text files that are deposited onto a user's device when they visit the Site, enabling it to identify and remember their device. These cookies retain information pertaining to the user's visit, encompassing details such as viewed content, language preferences, the timing and duration of each visit, and accessed advertisements.

    Cookies are useful because they allow the Site to recognize the User's device, improve the Site's performance, and track the User's activity on the Site.

    For Users, cookies are convenient because they allow you not to enter your login, password, and other data every time it is necessary to use the Site.

    3. StorageCopy

    Session storage files are automatically removed when you close your browser, while local storage is persistent and remains stored even after the browser is closed.

    Cookies can also be deleted from local storage either at the user's request or as a consequence of actions taken by the User.

    4. What kind of cookies do we use?
    Technical or functional cookies Copy

    4.1. Some cookies ensure that certain parts of the Site work as expected and your User preferences are known. By placing functional cookies, we make it easier for you to visit our Site. This way you do not have to enter the same information over and over when visiting our Site.

    4.2. We may place these cookies without your consent.

    4.3. Technical cookies allow the Site to remember your choices (such as your Username, language, or region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personalized features. These cookies can also be used to remember changes you make to text size, fonts, and other parts of web pages that you can customize, and to remember the decisions you make to improve your experience. Session cookies have no expiration date. Instead, they are only kept for as long as the browser or tab is open. As soon as the browser is closed, they are irretrievably lost. This type of cookie is used, for example, to store the User's bank credentials while navigating their bank's site, as their information will be forgotten as soon as the tab is closed.

    4.4. The technical cookies that we process include:

    The User's IP address;

    Browser and operating system version;

    Device type;

    Browser language.

    5. Statistical or behavioral cookies
    Technical or functional cookies Copy

    5.1. We use statistics cookies to optimize the Site experience for our Users.

    5.2. With the help of these statistical cookies, we get an idea of the use of our Site.

    5.3. We ask for your permission to place statistics cookies.

    5.4. Statistical cookies are only used to improve the performance of the Site. These cookies determine which pages are used and help us analyze data about web page traffic and improve our Site in order to tailor it to customer needs.

    5.5. The statistical cookies that we process include:

    Page scrolling data;

    Data of transitions on hyperlinks;

    Button click data;

    Text selection and copying data;

    User consent to the provision of cookies.

    5.6. Before using the Site, User gives his consent to the processing of cookies.

    5.7. User shall confirm that he or she has read this Policy before giving his consent to the processing of certain cookies.

    6. Your rights when processing cookies
    Technical or functional cookies Copy

    6.1. You have the following rights regarding the processing of your cookies:

    You have the right to understand why cookies are necessary, what their purpose is, and their duration.

    Right to access: You are entitled to access the cookies that we are aware of.

    Right to rectification: You have the right to modify, rectify, delete, or block your cookies at any time.

    Right to data portability: You have the right to request all your cookies from one operator and transfer them entirely to another operator.

    Right to object: You can express your objection to the processing of your cookies.

    We will take appropriate action if there is no reasonable basis for processing cookies.

    7. Enabling/disabling and deleting cookies
    Technical or functional cookies Copy

    7.1. You can use your internet browser to delete cookies automatically or manually.

    7.2. You have the option to delete cookies from your internet browser either automatically or manually.

    7.3. You can specify that certain cookies should not be placed, or you can adjust your internet browser settings to receive a notification each time a cookie is set. For detailed instructions, please refer to the help section of your browser. Additionally, you can delete cookies by clearing your browser history.

    7.4. Data stored in local storage is deleted within three months after you cease using the Site.

    7.5. Users can also disable or entirely remove cookies by adjusting their browser settings.

    7.6. To configure your browser settings, read the instructions for configuring your browser settings.

    7.7. It is important to note that the Site may not function correctly if all cookies or essential technical cookies are disabled. If you delete cookies from your browser, they will be reinstated after your consent when you revisit the Site.
